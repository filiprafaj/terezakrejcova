Biography

Tereza is a contemporary dancer based in Salzburg, Austria. Comming from Czech Republic, her first encounter with contemporary dance happened in Duncan Centre Conservatory in Prague, where she completed six years of study with six months internship in Fontys School of Fine and Performing Arts in Tilburg, Netherlands. Desiring to dive deeper into dance studies, she joined SEAD Salzburg Experimentl Academy of Dance, where she is currently enrolled as a fourth year student. Tereza performed in many pieces by various makers such as opera Intolleranza 1960 by Belgian director Jan Lauers, What now? By Danish theatre director Anna Balslev, A Body from Japanese butoh dancer Min Tanaka or Pulsar by Czech choreographer Michal Zahora and many other works by guest teachers she met at SEAD. Tereza is a dedicated performer who considers her role on stage as a transmitter through which the energy is chanelled… Besides performing, she is also highly interested in bringing not only other people‘s visions alive, but her owns. She has created two solo performances Uncovered and Naturmela in cooperation with musician Vaclav Chalupsky and The Gingerbread House as a part of Choreography Major programme at SEAD. Essential aspect of her work is… She considers body as… Crutial part of her lifestyle is a strong relation and admiration of nature, where she wills to spends as much time as her work allows for she believes it balances her life and fills her with energy, inspiration and peace.
